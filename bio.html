Jiaji Huang – Biography

Short Bio

Jiaji Huang is a senior research scientist and research lead at Baidu Research, Silicon Valley AI Lab. He received a Bachelor’s degree from University of Science and Technology of China in 2011, and a PhD degree from Duke University in 2016. Both degrees are in electrical engineering. His PhD works mostly focus on statistical signal processing and machine learning, with a special interest in understanding how geometry of high dimensional space impacts various tasks, e.g., signal reconstruction, representation and classification.

His recent research interest is mainly in computational linguistics and natural language processing, especially problems that are less driven by the amount of supervised data. For example, language modeling and word embedding are two very recent and representative directions he is working on. He is always fascinated by the geometry of word/phrase embedding space, and its connection with semantics.

He has been actively publishing as first author in several top conferences/journals, including NeurIPS, ICCV, EMNLP, ACL, IEEE Transaction on Signal Processing and SIAM journal on Imaging Sciences. He has an Erdős number of 3.
